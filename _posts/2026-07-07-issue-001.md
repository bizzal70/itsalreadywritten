## This Week's Verdict

The industry spent the week doing paperwork: ENnie nominees posted, an expo shoved a year down the calendar, and the discourse settled into the eternal question of whether a ten-foot pole earns its slot in the pack. If you were hoping for errata, you will leave hungry. Bring bundles.

## Releases & Announcements

The **2026 ENnie nominees** are out, per **EN World**, which also carried the week's practical bread and butter: another **Freebies, Sales, and Charity Bundles** roundup spanning **D&D**, **Star Trek Adventures**, **Free League**, **Paranoia**, **Shadowrun**, and assorted OSR.

On the product side, **WizKids** put out a **D&D Spirit Board**, a physical replica of the Ravenloft magic item, which is either charming or the most inevitable merchandising decision of the year. **MonkeyDM's** *Steinhardt's Guide to the Eldritch Hunt* now has player-facing content live on **D&D Beyond**. **Beadle & Grimms** will happily sell you the Silver Edition of *The Horrors Within* for $225.

Third-party and indie output continued at its usual clip. **Icarus Games** announced *A Night of Adventure Vol. 1*, seven short 5e adventures for the prep-starved. **Philip Reed Games** shipped *Stranger Danger for Shadowdark RPG*, over 200 NPC hooks for the barmaid your party will interrogate for twenty minutes. **ND Hobbies** released *Black Jam*, a post-apocalyptic OSR game built on **The Black Hack**. **Bat in the Attic** put out a Maps and Reference Sheets book for the Northern Marches. **EN World's** print news column noted a new **Warhammer: The Old World RPG** alongside supplements for *Áth Cliath*, *Star Trek Adventures*, and a **Spelljammer** AD&D 2E item, plus D&D 5.5 material.

A note for the trade watchers: **ICv2** reports RPGs were up in a quiet spring, which is the kind of sentence that keeps distributors calm.

## Rules & Errata

Nothing official this week. **Wizards of the Coast** ran its first quarterly "season" model in 2026, per a German-language thread from **Arkanil**, wrapping *Season of Horror* (April through June) as a themed bundle of core book, store program, and digital extras rather than standalone releases. That is a publishing structure, not a rules change, but it is the closest thing to news out of the D&D machine this week. **Shadowdark** and **DCC** produced no errata. Read the book you already own.

## The Community

The eternal debates ran on schedule. **Lair of Secrets** polled whether the ten-foot pole deserves its place in the adventuring kit, then went on to geek out about polearms in D&D **Unearthed Arcana**, so at least the commitment is consistent. **Thoughtpunks** posted an earnest defense of rules-light systems against their usual critics, part of a small run of posts on what the author calls "dice modes," the idea that dice simulate, narrate, or answer questions depending on the game.

**Ruckerworks** argued that players and DMs across D&D, **Shadowdark**, **Daggerheart**, and **Old-School Essentials** all want the same thing, namely cool characters, which is hard to dispute and easy to publish. The OSR crowd stayed busy: **flyrefi** posted an OSR/NSR survey analysis, **Xenograg** excerpted the perennial "as armor improved, swords became less effective" argument, and **Seed of Worlds** ran capsule reviews of the **Kala Mandala** zine line. A character generator for **Dolmenwood** surfaced at dlmnwd.com. **MattyBoomBatty** reviewed *Return of the Ripper* for DCC. And **randomwizard** asked the only question that matters: how many war dogs may a party buy.

## On the Horizon

**Sly Flourish** launched the Kickstarter for *Rise of the Lazy Gamemaster*, complete with Patreon discounts and a downloadable preview, which is your reminder that even the calm ones fundraise. **So Many Robots** entered the final three days of *Machines of Bone & Blood*, a necroindustrial 5e campaign already past a dozen stretch goals. **Nerdronomicon** continued crowdfunding *The Sorcerer's Conundrum*, a level-2 DCC adventure zine.

Further out, **EN World** flags *Merryshire Detective Club*, a Hobbit-meets-Midsomer game whose crowdfunder is about three weeks away. Less happily, **London's D&D Fan Expo 2026** has been postponed to September 2027, which is a long time to hold a ticket.
