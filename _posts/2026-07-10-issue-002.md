## This Week's Verdict

Gen Con approaches, so **Wizards of the Coast** is assembling a six-floor tower, a livestreamed keynote, and a preorder wall labeled **Arcana Unleashed**. Everyone else is running Shadowdark quests, arguing about race-as-class, and finding their 1983 RPGA pins in a drawer. Business as usual, then, with more scaffolding.

## Releases & Announcements

The headline this week is **Wizards of the Coast**'s **Arcana Unleashed**, the high-magic 5e supplement now confirmed to carry 8 subclasses, 33 spells, 10 backgrounds, over 20 feats, and over 45 magic items. Its companion adventure **Arcana Unleashed: Deadfall** is a Level 11-20 campaign centered on the Red Wizards of Thay, with an accompanying **Arcana Unleashed Map Pack** (10 physical and digital maps). Both books are up for preorder and slated for September 2026.

On the smaller-press side, **The Solo Player's Companion for Shadowdark** launched in PDF (reported via **The Gaming Gang**), and **EN World**'s RPG Print News rounded up supplements for **Dragonbane**, **Shadowdark**, and **Mausritter**, alongside ruin-themed dice and, for reasons known only to their maker, RPG-scented tea candles for **Ten Candles**. On the OSR shelf, **BX Advanced Bestiary, Volume 3** adds monster variants and player options.

Elsewhere in the wider hobby: **Daggerheart** opened playtesting for a Summoner class with Necromancer and Theurgist subclasses. And **Space Ghost Annual 2026** #1 got a preview at **ICv2**, if that is your corner of the table.

## Rules & Errata

Nothing official this week. The closest thing to a rules development is **Daggerheart**'s Summoner playtest, which is a proposal rather than errata, and the **Arcana Unleashed** subclass reveals, which are marketing rather than corrections. No 5e, Shadowdark, or DCC errata crossed the desk.

## The Community

The best of the OSR discourse came from **The Rpg Gazette**, whose "Race-as-Class and the Quiet Death of a Design Idea" is worth your time if you have ever argued about whether "elf" should be a class. Related in spirit, **Azukail Games**' Tabletop Mercenary podcast asked the perennial question "Why Do So Many TTRPG Products Use D&D 5E?", which answers itself if you glance at the preorder queue above.

Old-timer energy ran high. A thread starting with **deinol** picked at AD&D's to-hit matrices living in the DMG as "Dungeon Master Only" material, with **mdhughes** surfacing archived TSR character sheets and a familiar grievance about THAC0. **GoldenGrognard** found a 1983 RPGA membership pin, and **randomwizard** waxed fond over O2 Blade of Vengeance, the one-DM-one-player module he loves and would recommend to no one. The honest coda: most groups "just want to sit around, maybe drink a beer, tell some nerdy jokes, beat up some orcs, collect gold, and call it a night."

For practical table advice, **Sly Flourish** reminded everyone to spread monster threat across all characters, and **The Alexandrian** reposted an ex-RPGNet review of A1 Slave Pits of the Undercity. On the play-report front, **kyonshi**'s Glimmermark session 55 delivered the week's finest reminder that shadows are not undead, a fact several failed Turn Undead rolls established the hard way.

## On the Horizon

The dominant near-term event is **Gen Con 2026**. **Wizards of the Coast** is going, per **EN World**, "all out," with special guests, a keynote livestreamed on YouTube on July 30th at 6 PM ET, and a six-floor "D&D Tower" (**ICv2** and **DnDFanatics** both have details) promising gaming, celebrities, and panels.

Crowdfunding remains busy. **So Many Robots**' necro-industrial 5e adventure **Machines of Bone & Blood** entered its final days having cleared a dozen stretch goals. **Kobold Press** is running **Pirates & Plunder: 5E Seafaring Options** for D&D and Tales of the Valiant on BackerKit and Kickstarter. **Sly Flourish** launched the Kickstarter for **Rise of the Lazy Gamemaster**. And per **Geek Native**'s Routinely Itemised roundup, the headline OSR campaign is Mikael "Oldtimer" Börjesson's Dragonbane **Return to Twin Mountains**, among others.

Smaller works in progress worth a note: **Scroll for Initiative** teased a new project, "The Quintessential Guide to Monsters," and various convention dates are firming up, from **KantCon** in Kansas to **MonsteriaCon** in Münster on September 12th. Plan accordingly, or do not; the books you already own are not going anywhere.
