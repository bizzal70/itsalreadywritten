*Issue #003 · Week of July 17, 2026*

## This Week's Verdict

A quiet week where the two headline stories are a crowdfunding launch and a legal footnote finally sprouting code. **Deathbringer** wants your money for brutal sword and sorcery, a community fork wants to build a free **5e** without **Wizards of the Coast**, and someone wants you to stat Pokemon as poison armor for the fourth straight day. Read your stat blocks twice, as they say.

## Releases & Announcements

**STARBEARDS**, a sci-fi space-dwarf setting for **Savage Worlds Adventure Edition**, landed on **DriveThruRPG** this week, with **starhawk** crediting writing, design, and editing work and flagging it as no-AI. Rock and stone.

**DriveThruRPG's** "Christmas in July" sale is doing its annual rounds. Among the discounted titles: the **Forgotten Realms Wiki's** own *Juniper's Companion to Venturesome Cookery* at 30 percent off, and two titles from **Rock Clan Productions**. On the licensed-property front, **ICv2** reports *Call of Cthulhu: Horror on the Orient Express - The Board Game* is heading to retail, and **Greg Pak** is taking over *Thundercats* with a new story arc.

**Kobold Press** continued its *Memoirs of a Lich* series. Nothing here reorders the shelf, but the shelf is at least being restocked.

## Rules & Errata

No official errata from any of the majors this week. The closest thing to a rules story is structural: following the SRD's move into **Creative Commons**, a free **5e** implementation is being developed and promoted under the **Free5e** banner via **Wyrmworks Publishing**. **GoldenGrognard** framed it in Ben Milton's "Corporate D&D versus Folk D&D" terms, hoping a widely-adopted free version cuts **Wizards of the Coast** out of the loop entirely. Worth watching, though "being developed" is doing a lot of work in that sentence.

Otherwise the practical rules content came from the usual quarter. **Sly Flourish** offered the eternal reminder to read your monster stat blocks twice and understand them before the table does, which pairs nicely with **Nat1Advice's** observation that the best counter to a player who has memorized the Monster Manual is a GM who has opened the Player's Handbook. **Shadowdark, DCC**, and the rest saw no official changes.

## The Community

The week's actual discourse was **Deathbringer**, and specifically **Professor Dungeon Master's** involvement, which **EN World** picked up and **Magnolia Keep** critiqued in a post titled "The Thoughtlessness of Deathbringer's Sales Pitch." So there is at least one real argument happening around the game rather than just the marketing copy.

Elsewhere, prep and reading dominated. **martin** posted a thorough read-through of **The Waking of Willowby Hall** for **Knave**, praising its interactivity, the hard-to-improvise details like library books and gallery paintings, and the Yoon-Suin vase cameo, while working out how abandoned the hall actually is. **takeonrules** wrote up returning to **PbtA** by way of **Stonetop**, and **enfors** put together thoughts on designing secrets in any system: give them an active keeper, make revelation change the world, and make disclosure an interesting choice. **The Alexandrian** continued its ex-RPGNet review series with *A2 - Secret of the Slavers Stockade*.

Two review blogs from **rlyehreviews** covered *Atomic Shadows* from **Wallhalla Games**, a post-apocalyptic game built on **Shadowdark**, and **Necrotic Gnome's** *Old-School Essentials Adventure Anthology 2*. And **ForesterDesigns** continued the daily Pokemon-armor magic item march across **5e** and **PF2e**, which is either dedication or a hostage situation.

## On the Horizon

**Deathbringer** is live on **BackerKit**, pitched as brutal sword-and-sorcery action. **Sly Flourish's** *Rise of the Lazy Gamemaster* Kickstarter is running and reportedly had the strongest opening days of any of his campaigns.

Two dates worth marking: **DCC Day** is July 18th, with **Goodman Games** open tables and swag at venues including **DMen Tap** in Chicago. **Tabletop Scotland 2026** has its **D&D Adventurers League** schedule up, including con-written adventures from the **Polyhedral Adventures** team. For the free-and-outdoors crowd, *The Anvil of Winter* by **Seba, Rackhir**, and **simiofdoom** is out on itch as an **OSR** pointcrawl submitted to the **Appendix N Jam**, complete with yaks, a dead god, and a spaceship.
